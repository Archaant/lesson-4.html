Artificial Intelligence (AI) has transitioned from a futuristic concept to an integral part of contemporary society, influencing various sectors including technology, healthcare, finance, and daily life. The journey of AI from theoretical discussions to practical applications highlights its transformative impact and the profound implications it holds for the future. The roots of AI can be traced back to the mid-20th century, with pioneers such as Alan Turing and John McCarthy laying the foundational theories and terminology. Turing's work, particularly his concept of the "Turing Test," posed fundamental questions about machine intelligence and its capacity to exhibit human-like reasoning. The term "artificial intelligence" was coined in 1956 during the Dartmouth Conference, marking the formal beginning of AI research. Early AI research focused on symbolic AI, where intelligence was perceived as a set of rules and logical operations. Despite initial optimism, the limitations of this approach led to periods of reduced funding and interest, known as "AI winters." These periods of stagnation were followed by renewed enthusiasm as researchers developed new techniques and computational power advanced.

Paragraph 1.3 The HTML <pre> element

The HTML <pre> element defines preformatted text. The text inside a <pre> element is displayed in a fixed-width font (usually Courier), and it preserves both spaces and line break. In the next paragraph we will give a demonstration.

<pre> 
Faith is wisdom.
Faith is power.
Faith is heart-fragrance.
Faith is the sun-child Of the Beyond.
</pre>
